BSE Equation#

The Bethe-Salper Equations (BSE) allows computing neutral excitations for electron-hole interactions. Following the Ai-MBPT formalism [Marini et al., 2009, Sangalli et al., 2019] the exciton energies and wavefunctions, are obtained solving the BSE equation in the Tamm-Dancoff approximation including local field effects [Onida et al., 2002].

The BSE can be recast into an eigenvalue equation

()#\[\begin{split} \left(\varepsilon_{c\mathbf{k}}^{\mathrm{GW}} -\varepsilon_{\mathrm{v}\mathbf{k-Q}}^{\mathrm{GW}}\right) A_{\mathrm{vc}\mathbf{k}}^\lambda+\sum_{\mathbf{k}^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{v}^{\prime}} K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{\mathbf{Q}} A_{v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}^{\lambda,\mathbf{Q}} =E_{\lambda,\mathbf{Q}} A_{v c \mathbf{k}}^{\lambda,\mathbf{Q}} \end{split}\]

where \(\varepsilon_{c\mathbf{k}/v\mathbf{k}}\) are quasi-particle band energies, \(A^{\lambda}_{v c\mathbf{k}}\) are the BSE coefficients, \(E_{\lambda,\mathbf{Q}}\) %are the energy of exciton \(\lambda\), and \(\mathbf{Q}\) the momentum transfer between an electron at \(\mathbf{k}\) and a hole at \(\mathbf{k-Q}\). The kernel \(K\) contains the electron-hole Coulomb interaction matrix elements, which is the sum of the direct \(K^{d}\) and exchange \(K^{x}\) terms and can be written as $\( K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{\mathbf{Q}} = K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{d,\mathbf{Q}} + K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{x,\mathbf{Q}} \)$

Following the prescription of \textit{Dias et al.} [Dias et al., 2023]} we can compute the direct and exchange terms as follows:

()#\[\begin{split} K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{d,\mathbf{Q}} = V(\mathbf{k}-\mathbf{k}^\prime)\langle c,\mathbf{k}| c^\prime,\mathbf{k}^\prime\rangle\langle v^\prime, \mathbf{k}^\prime-\mathbf{Q}|v\mathbf{k-Q}\rangle \\ K_{\substack{\mathrm{vc\mathbf{k}} \\ v^{\prime} c^{\prime} \mathbf{k}^{\prime}}}^{x,\mathbf{Q}} = -V(\mathbf{k}-\mathbf{k}^\prime)\langle c,\mathbf{k}| v,\mathbf{k}-\mathbf{Q}\rangle\langle v^\prime, \mathbf{k}^\prime-\mathbf{Q}|c^\prime\mathbf{k}^\prime-\mathbf{Q}\rangle \\ \end{split}\]
